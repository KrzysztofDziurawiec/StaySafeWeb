Fire safety is a set of practices that greatly reduce the destruction caused by fire.

Fire Prevention

Equipment

Have the equipment serviced/checked by an expert or competent person to prevent sparks and fires Proper housekeeping e.g. preventing ventilation points becoming clogged with dust or other material

Electrical Safety

All electrical equipment and installations designed installed, maintained, protected and used to prevent danger Get a qualified electrical contractor to install and/or repair electrical equipment Try to maintain effective pest control to avoid rodent damage to electric wiring and equipment Remove defective electrical equipment Use extensions cords safely e.g. do not put it under the carpet or across walking areas Use only one device per outlet

Arson

Based on a study called “Causes of fire disasters in Secondary Schools in Kenya” written by Pamela Imisa Shibutse, Prof. China S and Dr. Ormuterema S (source) it is known that arson is a common occurrence (42%) in secondary schools based in Vihiga County. It is really hard to prevent arson, but here are some points that can reduce that risk:

Discourage unauthorised entry to the site by the use of signs
Presence of work/school staff
Intruder alarms to prevent unauthorised entry into the building
Reduce the opportunity for an offender to start a fire e.g. place bins/re-cycling containers at least 8 metres from the building
Reduce the scope for potential fire damage e.g. have an early warning of the outbreak of fire
Keep flammable items properly stored and secured learn more

Flammable materials

Identify all flammable materials
Replace high flammable materials with less flammable ones
Reduce the amount of flammable materials to the smallest amount possible

Cleanliness & housekeeping

Never store flammable rubbish in escape routes
Storage rooms should be regularly inspected and cleared of flammable materialslearn more

Limiting the development of fire

Cut off any fire’s oxygen supply e.g. close windows and doors where possible
Fighting the fire with the correct extinguisher (if it is safe to do so)

Different types of fires and how to put them out

Fire class table:

Storage fires

Smoother with blanket or rug to cut off the air Use type A extinguisher for papers Use type B extinguisher for rags, liquids and hair spray/glue




Electrical fires

Switch off appliance and pull out plug Try to extinguish a fire with Type C extinguisher Never try to cool it with water


Cooking fires

Shut off oven or stove Use Type B Extinguisher

Quiz

1. What is Fire Safety?

  • Making sure fire is safe
  • How to be safe around fire
  • Set of practices that greatly reduce the destruction of fire
  • None of the above

2. How to prevent equipment fire?

  • Make sure that the equipment is off at all times
  • Never check if the equipment is defected
  • Do not use the equipment so it doesn’t overhea
  • Have the equipment regularly checked by an expert or competent person

3. How to prevent electrical fire?

  • Use as much devices you can per outlet
  • Always install and repair electrical equipment by yourself
  • Put extension cords under the carpet
  • Get a qualified electrical contractor to install and/or repair electrical equipment

4. How to prevent arson fire?

  • Encourage unauthorised entry into the building
  • Never use signs
  • Keep flammable items properly stored and secured
  • Do not use any form of alarms e.g. intruder alarms

5. How to prevent flammable material fire?

  • Make sure there are a lot of flammable items in the building
  • Reduce the amount of flammable materials to the smallest amount possible
  • Replace low flammable materials with high flammable ones
  • All of the above

6. How to limit the development of fire?

  • Leave all of the doors and windows open
  • Never use fire extinguisher
  • Keep all doors and windows closed
  • Try reducing the development of fire using your hands

7. How to put out storage fire?

  • Smoother with paper to cut off the air
  • Use type A or/and B extinguisher according to the type of fire
  • Use type S extinguisher
  • Use type C extinguisher

8. How to put out electrical fire?

  • Keep the plug in the outlet
  • Try to cool it with water
  • Use type C extinguisher
  • Use type B extinguisher

9. How to put out cooking fire?

  • Keep the stove on until the fire is gone
  • Use type C extinguisher
  • Use type B extinguisher
  • Use type A extinguisher

10. Which step does apply when using fire extinguisher?

  • Pull the trigger
  • Squeeze the pin
  • Hold the extinguisher under your armpit
  • Sweep from side to side

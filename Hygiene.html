Personal hygiene is a routine of personal care that keeps you clean and healthy. It involves regular care of your hair, skin face, teeth, ears, hands, nails and feet. If you follow hygiene practices correctly, it will reduce the spread of infectious diseases.

General Practices

- Washing the body often. If possible, have a shower or bath every day. If you do not have access to shower or bath, wash your body with wet sponge or cloth.
- Change into clean clothes and clean/wash them regularly. Also hanging clothes in the sun helps to kill some disease-causing germs and parasites.
- Turning away from other people or covering your mouth with hand or cloth when coughing or sneezing will reduce the risk of spreading illnesses.

Hygiene Practices - Hair

If you happen to have oily hair, it is important to note that you should wash your hair with shampoo or soap at least once per week.

Source: here

Hygiene Practices - Teeth

Brushing
It is advised to brush your teeth after each meal, or at least 3 times a day. Brushing removes the plaque that causes tooth decay or cavities. It also helps keep gums healthy and breath fresh.

Flossing
Flossing should be done every time teeth are brushed. It will help with preventing teeth cavity and gum disease.

Source: here

Hygiene Practices - Ears

A healthy ear will naturally clean itself, but it is important to look out for potentially harmfulconditions. For example earwax is a natural protector of the inner ear canal against dust, insects or other small particles.

However if there is an excessive amount of earwax in your ear and it causes discomfort, it can be necessary to carefully remove it using assistive tools such as cotton buds.

Source: here

Hygiene Practices - Hands

Hand hygiene is the most important measure to avoid the transmission of harmful germs and prevent spread of infectious diseases.

You should wash your hands with soap and water every time after going to the toilet. Also you should wash them if they are visibly dirty.

Hygiene Practices - Feet

8 steps of how to keep your feet clean and healthy:
1. Clean your feet – Try to clean your feet with warm water and soap daily.
2. Dry your feet – Use a towel to dry your feet.
3. Apply talcum powder if possible – After washing and drying your feet, put a thin coating of talcum or some medicinal powder.
4. Wear cotton socks – Avoid synthetic material on your feet as it restricts the flow of air.
5. Air your feet regularly – When possible, remove your footwear an expose your feet to the air.
6. Keep your toenails short – Maintain your toenails.
7. Inspect your feet regularly – Regular inspection of feet will alert you to swelling, blistering, cuts and blisters.
8. Exfoliate and Moisturize – Soak your feet in water and use pumice stone to scrub your feet.
